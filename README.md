# capstone-project

## Project Title
Sleep deprivatin effects on adolscents 

## Overview
This project investigates how important sleep is, especially in adolescents.

## ❓ Research Question
How does sleep deprivation affect memory, focus, and academic performance in teenagers?

## Different Effetcs
- Effects on the Brain and Learning

During sleep, the brain processes and stores information learned during the day.
If teens don’t sleep enough, they may experience:
Poor memory
Difficulty concentrating in class
Slower thinking and problem-solving
Lower academic performance
Sleep helps the brain organize memories, which is important for studying and learning new material.

- Daytime Tiredness

Lack of sleep often causes:
Feeling very tired during school
Difficulty staying awake
Less motivation to participate in class
This can make learning much harder.

- Effects on Mood and Mental Health

Sleep deprivation can also affect emotions.
Adolescents who don’t get enough sleep may experience:
Irritability
Higher stress levels
Mood swings
Increased risk of anxiety or sadness

- Effects on Physical Health

Sleep is also important for the body’s development.
Not getting enough sleep may lead to:
Weakened immune system (getting sick more often)
Low energy
Problems with growth and overall health
Why Adolescents Often Don’t Get Enough Sleep
Teenagers naturally have a later body clock, a shift related to the scientific concept called Circadian rhythm.
Because of this:
Teens feel sleepy later at night
But they still have to wake up early for school

Other factors include:
Phones and social media
Homework
Gaming or watching videos

## Stages of Sleep
1. Light sleep N1

Dozing off phase, lasting only for a while, heart rate & brain slows down. Alpha brain waves shift into theta waves. So we end up waking up easily in this phase.
2. Deeper light sleep N2

Deep sleep but not dreaming yet. The cycle lasts for 20 min. Body temp drops, brain eases down, heart rate slows down too. Learning and memory. “Brian’s housekeeping stage” 
3. Deep sleep N3

Your brain shows slow delta waves, the body focuses on repair, growth hormones, surge, muscles heal, and immune systems heal for recovery. Crucial for feeling refreshed. No deep sleep= no energy.
4. REM sleep

90 minutes after you fall asleep. This is when you dream. Vivid dreams happen here, but it also strengthens learning, creativity, and emotional processing.
